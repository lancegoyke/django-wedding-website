The Proposal

Lance and Allison got engaged on July 18th, 2020 in Mountain View, California. And the proposal is based off of three inside jokes: opal, The Legend of Korra, and Lance's love for pens.

And it's extra unique because Allison proposed to Lance.

In October 2018, Lance took a business course which placed he and his cohort in a group named "Opal". How exotic.

Excited for the course, Lance purchased a tiny opal elephant figurine. Elephants are known for their good memory, and the opal stone's translucent, multicolored appearance is beautiful.

When it arrived, Allison inquired about it as if to say, "Are you a collector of fine jewelry now, Lance?"

"This is my oh-PAUL elephant. It's the name of my cohort."

Confused, Allison asked for clarification: "Oh-paul? How do you spell that?"

"O-P-A-L."

"Lance, that's pronounced OH-pull."

She got quite the kick out of that. And never forgot it. Lance's mom even bought her an opal ring a year later when my family came to visit. And his fake proposal was not well-received. Luckily he didn't oversell it ðŸ˜…

Some year later, we watched the masterpiece animated television show The Legend of Korra (spoilers approaching). During the show, there's a male character with a close female partner. Every time he needs something, he asks her to "do the thing"; she always knows what he means.

At the end of the show, he kneels down, presents her a ring, and asks,"Will you do the thing for the rest of our lives?" [watch clip]

For whatever reason, Lance has always been fascinated with nice pens and has built quite a collection.

With a quick Google search, Allison found a fountain pen made out of opal stones, also known as The perfect engagement gift. She left Lance an opal pen and a post-it note that said, "Will you do the thing for the rest of our lives?"

Lance came to wake her up and say, "Yes." Then thanked her for the introvert's perfect proposal.
